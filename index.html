About Me

Hello, and thank you for visiting my page. I have some items, including my resume and links to some projects I've done, contained within. This website serves as a living document of my continued body of work. I hope that you enjoy some of its contents.

My name is Tito. I grew up in Southern California outside Los Angeles. I have traveled all over the world and am an avid outdoor enthusiast. When I'm not programming or reading, you can usually find me on a trail somewhere or climbing with friends. Presently I reside in the beautiful Pacific Northwest with my Life Partner, two furry cats, and an incredible network of friends - many of whom our programmers like myself.

I'm interested in Cloud Performance Engineering and I'm presently interning for a team developing database observability tools from the hardware to application stack. In addition to my Masters program, I also am working on Linux Cloud Certificates. Specifically, working in Docker, Kubernetes, and becoming proficient in the Linux ecosystem.

I'm also interested in full-stack development, even though this is the first website I have ever built. I like the different elements working in concert with each other, and hope to bring my knowledge of working in backend APIs to front-end web development. Ultimately my goal is to finish up my Masters in Computer Science, work a few years developing back-end cloud technologies, and work cross-functionally with other stakeholders to deliver high value products to the marketplace.

I view Full-Stack application development and back-end performance engineering (The Cloud) as fundamentally 2 sides of the same coin. They are not mutually exclusive amongst development teams, though it seems that in my interactions with software engineers, they are treated seperately. My interest in full-stack web development is to be able to work cross-functionally with application developers, whose workload demands vary, in order to design better, observable cloud systems that can drive continuous delivery among a variety of dynamic platforms.
